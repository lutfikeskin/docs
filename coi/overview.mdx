Insurance Doc Intelligence lets you generate ACORD 25 Certificates of Insurance (COIs) directly from policy data you have already saved in the system. Instead of manually re-entering coverage details, limits, and insured information into a PDF form, you select the policy, specify the certificate holder, confirm which coverage sections apply, and download a ready-to-send COI in seconds. This eliminates transcription errors and keeps every COI consistent with your on-file policy record.

## How It Works

Generating a COI follows the same core steps whether you are creating one certificate or a hundred:

1. **Select the policy** — search for the insured by name or policy number and click the policy row to load it.
2. **Choose a certificate holder** — enter the holder's name and address manually, or quick-fill from your saved company list.
3. **Configure coverage sections** — toggle General Liability, Automobile Liability, and Motor Truck Cargo on or off depending on what the policy actually covers.
4. **Download the PDF** — click **Generate COI** and save the file.

The ACORD 25 template is pre-loaded in the system; you never need to upload or configure it.

## What Goes on the COI

Every COI is populated directly from the policy record. The following fields are pulled automatically:

- **Insurer name** — uses the legal entity name (`underwriter_name`) when it is available; falls back to the carrier brand name (`carrier_name`) when it is not
- **Insured name and address** — including city, state, and ZIP
- **Policy number**
- **Effective date and expiration date** — formatted as MM/DD/YYYY
- **Coverage sections** — General Liability, Automobile Liability, and Motor Truck Cargo (each only printed when enabled)
- **Limits** — per-occurrence, general aggregate, combined single limit, and cargo limit/deductible as applicable
- **Certificate holder name and address** — including city, state, and ZIP

You can edit the insured name, address, and NAIC number directly on the Create COI page before generating, so minor corrections do not require you to update the underlying policy record first.

## Coverage Sections

The COI form includes three coverage sections that you can toggle independently:

| Section | Description |
|---|---|
| **General Liability (GL)** | Covers bodily injury and property damage. When enabled, you can also select the general aggregate limit ($1,000,000 or $2,000,000). |
| **Automobile Liability** | Covers commercial auto. Uses the same policy number and dates as the GL section. |
| **Motor Truck Cargo** | Covers cargo in transit. Populated from the cargo limit and deductible on the policy. |

Coverage sections are **not assumed to be enabled**. Each toggle is pre-set based on whether the system found evidence of that coverage when the policy was extracted — `has_general_liability`, `has_auto_liability`, and a non-empty cargo limit. Always verify the pre-set values match the actual policy before generating.

## File Naming

Generated COIs follow a consistent naming pattern in both single and bulk flows:

```
COI - Insured Name - Certificate Holder Name.pdf
```

Characters that are invalid in Windows filenames (`\ / : * ? " < > |`) are automatically replaced with spaces, and extra whitespace is collapsed. This makes COI files easy to identify and sort whether they are saved individually or extracted from a bulk ZIP.
