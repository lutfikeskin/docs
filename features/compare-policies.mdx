The Compare Policies page gives you a structured, field-by-field diff between any two policies in your database. The most common use case is reviewing a renewal quote against the expiring policy — you want to know instantly whether the carrier changed a limit, dropped a coverage, added a vehicle, or adjusted the premium. But Compare works just as well for rewrites, endorsement-driven replacements, or simply auditing two policies that look suspiciously similar.

Instead of printing two dec pages and highlighting differences by hand, you get a single screen that surfaces every change automatically.

## Selecting Policies

Two dropdown pickers sit side by side at the top of the page — **Policy A** on the left and **Policy B** on the right.

Each option in the dropdown is labeled with:

```
Insured Name — Carrier — Policy Number — exp [Expiration Date]
```

This label format gives you enough identity information to find the right record without opening individual policies. For example:

```
Acme Trucking — Progressive — CPP-112233 — exp Dec 31, 2025
```

You can compare **any two policies in the database**, regardless of which customer they belong to. Policy A and Policy B are completely independent pickers — there are no restrictions on type, carrier, or date range.

<Info>
  The Policy B picker automatically excludes whichever policy you selected for Policy A, so you can never accidentally compare a policy against itself.
</Info>

### Auto-pairing with a prior renewal

When you select Policy A and a confirmed renewal relationship exists in the database, a one-click **🔗 Auto-pair with prior renewal** button appears. Click it to instantly populate Policy B with the prior version. This is the fastest path to a renewal diff — no scrolling through dropdowns required.

If no confirmed relationship is found, a short caption tells you to pick Policy B manually.

## Reading the Diff

Once both policies are selected, a **Field-by-field comparison** table renders every scalar policy field as a row.

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Δ** | A 🟡 marker if the two values differ; blank if they are identical |
| **Field** | The human-readable field name (e.g., "Premium", "Liability Limit", "Expiration Date") |
| **Policy A** | The value from the first policy you selected |
| **Policy B** | The value from the second policy you selected |

Changed rows are sorted to the **top** of the table so you see what moved without scrolling past a long list of identical fields. Unchanged fields follow alphabetically — they're there for full context, not just the delta.

This means you can answer the most common renewal question — *"what exactly changed?"* — by reading the first few rows of the table.

## KPI Strip

Four metrics render between the pickers and the diff table whenever both policies are selected, giving you a quantified summary of the comparison before you read the details.

<CardGroup cols={4}>
  <Card title="Scalar fields changed" icon="pen-to-square">
    The count of individual policy fields (carrier, premium, limits, dates, etc.) where Policy A and Policy B differ.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Vehicles Δ" icon="truck">
    Shows how many vehicles are only on Policy A (removed) and how many are only on Policy B (added). Format: <code>+B / −A</code>.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Drivers Δ" icon="id-card">
    Same format as Vehicles Δ — additions on B and removals from A at a glance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Premium Δ" icon="circle-dollar-to-slot">
    The dollar difference in premium between Policy A and Policy B, plus the percentage change. An increase renders red (higher cost to insured) and a decrease renders green.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Coverage and Vehicle Differences

Below the scalar diff table, four expandable sections show collection-level differences. Each section automatically expands when there are additions or removals to call attention to.

<Accordion title="🚗 Vehicles">
  Three sub-sections: vehicles **only on Policy A** (dropped at renewal), vehicles **only on Policy B** (added at renewal), and vehicles **unchanged on both** policies. Each row shows Year, Make, Model, VIN, GVW, and vehicle type.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="🧑 Drivers">
  The same three-way split for drivers: only on A, only on B, and unchanged. Each row shows driver name, license number, and whether the driver is excluded.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="🛡️ Coverages">
  Four sub-sections for coverages:

  - **Limit changed** — the same coverage line appears on both policies but with different limits. Each row shows the A and B values side by side for per-occurrence, per-accident, CSL, aggregate, and deductible.
  - **Only on Policy A** — coverages dropped in Policy B
  - **Only on Policy B** — new coverages in Policy B
  - **Unchanged** — coverages identical on both policies
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="📎 Additional Interests">
  Loss payees, additional insureds, lienholders, and other named parties that appear only on A, only on B, or on both. Adding or removing a bank, lessor, or certificate holder mid-term is easy to miss — this section makes it explicit.
</Accordion>

<Note>
  If the two policies you selected belong to different customers, a warning banner appears at the top of the comparison:

  > ⚠️ These policies belong to different customers. Comparing across customers is allowed but is rarely what you want — make sure this is intentional.

  The comparison still runs fully and accurately. The warning is informational — it doesn't block or alter the diff results. Cross-customer comparisons are valid when you're evaluating a carrier rewrite that moved a client to a new policy number under a different entity name.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Make Compare your first stop whenever you receive a renewal quote. Open it from the [Renewals](/features/renewals) page using the **🔀 Compare to prior renewal** button on the action panel — it pre-fills both pickers automatically. Then read the KPI strip first for a one-second gut check, and drill into the scalar table and collection expanders only if something looks off.
</Tip>
