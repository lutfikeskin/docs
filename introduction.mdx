Insurance Doc Intelligence is an AI-powered operations workspace built for insurance brokers and agents who spend too much time manually keying data from policy PDFs. Upload a declarations page, and the application extracts structured policy data in seconds — populating coverage limits, effective dates, vehicle schedules, named insureds, and more. From there you can review and correct the results, save the record to your local database, generate a Certificate of Insurance, and track upcoming renewals — all without leaving a single browser tab.

## How It Works

The core workflow moves in five stages:

1. **Upload** — You drop a PDF declarations page onto the Process Policies screen. The app reads the raw text and passes it to the Gemini extraction pipeline.
2. **Extract** — The pipeline identifies the policy type, selects the matching prompt, and returns a structured JSON payload with a confidence score attached to every field.
3. **Review** — The extracted values appear on a form. Fields that fall below your configured confidence gate are pre-cleared so you can type them in manually rather than skimming past a wrong value. Nothing blocks you from saving — the gate is a nudge, not a lock.
4. **Save** — You click Save and the record lands in your local database, ready to search, export, or compare.
5. **COI** — Open Create COI, locate the policy, select a certificate holder from your holders list, and download the completed PDF.

Renewal tracking and policy comparison sit on top of the same database, so every record you save is immediately available across every view.

## What You Need

Before you launch the application, make sure you have:

- **Python 3.9 or later** installed on your machine or server.
- A **Google Gemini API key** — required for PDF extraction and natural-language database queries. You can browse the database and generate COIs from already-saved policies without one.
- The **application running locally or on a server** — either via `streamlit run app.py` on your machine or deployed as a container.
